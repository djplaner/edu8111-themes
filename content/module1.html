Theme 2 - Environment, ecology, and evolution

Introduction

Learning brings fundamental changes in human understandings and behaviours, whether our experience is formal, non-formal or informal. In each case the learner changes through the experience of interaction with elements of an educational setting, with aspects of the world, or with personal recollections. Interaction has long been recognised as central to educational design regardless of the underlying educational theory.  For example, Andersson and Andersson (2005) emphasise the importance of authentic and socially constructed learning, and Swan (2002) describes ways in which online design supports those processes. Environments (or contexts) influence user expectations and behaviours (Schwier, 2012).
